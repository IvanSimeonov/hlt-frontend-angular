Why calculating BMI is so important?

A person's body mass index (BMI) is calculated by dividing their weight in kilograms by their height in meters squared.

BMI is an inexpensive and easy screening approach for weight categoryâ€”underweight, healthy weight, overweight, and obesity. Although BMI doesn't measure body fat directly, it has a modest correlation with more direct measures of body fat, variety of metabolic and illness consequences.

BMI has several drawbacks, including the inaccuracy for pregnant women, those under the height of five feet, and those with very muscular builds. Age is also not taken into consideration.

Please keep in mind that your BMI is only a reference to assist in determining your weight goal. Discuss any concerns you have about your weight, weight loss, or modifications to your food and exercise routine with a doctor or other health care provider.
